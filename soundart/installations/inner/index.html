Inner

Inner is an interactive sculpture that encourages the audience in reflecting on its own body awareness and the research of our identity, our inner self. Trough touching heart rate sensors (the green small lights), the audience imprints its own inner self on the musical structure and light variations. The music makes the sculpture and its different materials resonate giving it a new identity. Synesthetic lighting underlines the organic, constructive and deconstructive characteristics of the sculpture. This installation is inspired from the junction of the research conducted by Balandino at Goldsmiths, University of London focused on prototyping new bio-musical instruments, and the artistic work of Victoria Curtin Rivera on abstract forms.
